Red flag, absolute contraindication for a CT examination.

Computer tomography (CT) involves potential exposure of both mother and fetus to ionising radiation. If accessible in a timely manner, other imaging techniques that do not involve electromagnetic radiation, such as ultrasound or magnetic resonance imaging (MRI), should be considered as a safer alternative to CT imaging during the first trimester of pregnancy.

Please discuss the indication for the CT examination with your doctor.

Red flag, possible contraindication for contrast medium application.

Most manufacturers of intravenous contrast advise that mothers should not breast-feed their babies for 24 to 48 hours after contrast medium is given. Please discuss the indication for the application of the contrast medium with your doctor.
